## Brand & Style

This design system is anchored in the concept of "Editorial Technology." It bridges the gap between the rigorous clarity of a research institution and the fluid innovation of a leading AI laboratory. The brand personality is intellectual, safe, and transparent—prioritizing human readability over technical flashiness.

The visual style is a refined mix of **Minimalism** and **Modern Editorial** design. It utilizes heavy whitespace to allow complex ideas room to breathe, treating digital interfaces with the same typographic care as a printed journal. The aesthetic rejects the "neon-and-dark-mode" tropes of traditional AI, opting instead for a tactile, grounded warmth that feels authoritative yet deeply approachable.

## Colors

The color palette is derived from natural, earthy tones to evoke a sense of stability and human presence. 

- **Primary (#D97757):** A muted ochre used sparingly for calls to action, focus states, and highlighting key insights. It provides warmth without the urgency of a standard "tech blue" or "alert red."
- **Neutral (#191919):** A deep charcoal used for all primary text and iconography, ensuring maximum contrast and legibility against the light backgrounds.
- **Surface Foundations:** The interface relies on a dual-tone background system. `#F9F8F0` (Off-white) serves as the primary canvas, while `#E6E1D3` (Warm Beige) is used for secondary containers, borders, and structural divisions.

Avoid pure blacks (#000) or pure whites (#FFF) to maintain the soft, paper-like quality of the interface.

## Typography

The typographic strategy uses a high-contrast pairing to distinguish between narrative and utility.

- **The Serif (Newsreader):** Used for all headlines and editorial storytelling. Its traditional, literary character conveys authority and long-form thought. Use generous top margins for headings to signal a change in topic.
- **The Sans (Manrope):** A precise, modern sans-serif used for body copy, navigation, and interface labels. It provides a clean, functional counterpoint to the serif.
- **Rhythm:** Line heights are intentionally generous (1.6-1.7 for body text) to reduce cognitive load and enhance the feeling of "openness" within the layout.

## Layout & Spacing

This design system utilizes a **Fixed Grid** model inspired by traditional typesetting. While the outer margins are fluid to accommodate different screen sizes, the content core is centered and constrained to prevent line lengths from becoming illegible.

- **Vertical Rhythm:** Use large-scale spacing (`lg` and `xl`) to separate major sections. The "breathability" of the site is its primary luxury.
- **Grid:** A 12-column grid is standard for desktop, but elements should often span the central 8 columns for long-form reading to maintain an editorial feel.
- **Padding:** Containers should use generous internal padding to ensure text never feels crowded by its borders.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth in this design system is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and **Low-Contrast Outlines** rather than traditional shadows. This maintains a flat, modernist aesthetic that feels integrated with the page.

- **The Tier System:** 
    - Level 0: The primary background (#F9F8F0).
    - Level 1: Inset or grouped content using the warm beige (#E6E1D3).
    - Level 2: Interactive elements or floating panels, defined by a subtle 1px border using a slightly darkened version of the beige.
- **Shadows:** Avoid drop shadows entirely. If depth must be signaled for a temporary overlay (like a modal), use a soft, large-radius "ambient" shadow with very low opacity (3-5%) tinted with the primary charcoal color.

## Shapes

The shape language is understated and professional. 

- **Corner Radius:** A "Soft" approach is used (4px/0.25rem). This provides a subtle hint of modern approachability without losing the architectural, structured feel of a sharp-edged layout.
- **Consistency:** Apply this radius to buttons, input fields, and cards. Larger containers like hero images may remain sharp (0px) to emphasize their scale and structural role in the layout.

## Components

Components should feel like "objects on a desk"—functional, tactile, and minimalist.

- **Buttons:** Use high-contrast "Primary" buttons with a Charcoal (#191919) background and Off-white text. Secondary buttons should be outlined with a 1px stroke.
- **Cards:** Cards should not have shadows. Use a subtle background shift to `#E6E1D3` or a fine 1px border. Focus on large typography inside cards to communicate value quickly.
- **Input Fields:** Use a minimalist "Bottom Border" style or a very light fully-framed stroke. The focus state should be indicated by a weight increase in the border or a shift to the primary ochre color.
- **Chips/Labels:** Use the `label-caps` typography style. Chips should have a light beige background and no border, keeping them unobtrusive.
- **Icons:** Use thin-stroke (1px or 1.5px) icons. They should be abstract and geometric, never illustrative or "bubbly."
- **Data Visualization:** Use the primary ochre and varying shades of charcoal/beige. Avoid bright, multi-colored charts; stick to a monochromatic or limited earthy palette to maintain the intellectual tone.
